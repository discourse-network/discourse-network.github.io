**AI tools** are rapidly changing how we write code and analyse data. While these tools offer clear benefits, their ad hoc adoption risks unintended consequences. In particular, the contributions of technical leaders those who guide projects to technical success may become less visible or undervalued, with implications for career satisfaction and progression. **This project investigates how AI** can be integrated into code development workflows responsibly, with a focus on sustaining code quality, professional skills, and the recognised value of technical leadership. 

**Specifically, we ask:**
 
- How can technical leaders use AI tools without compromising code quality, professional expertise, or job satisfaction?
 
- How can time savings from AI be used intentionally to enhance the visibility and impact of uniquely human contributions to research projects?

**To address these questions, we will:**

- Produce clear, practical guidance on responsible AI-assisted development for technical leaders.

- Design, pilot, and evaluate a structured template for AI-assisted projects. 

**To support this,** we will (i) produce clear, practical guidance on responsible AI-assisted development and (ii) design, pilot and evaluate a structured template for AI-assisted projects. This template will set out opportunities in the development workflow to use AI (e.g. planning, testing or code review) and paired strategies to mitigate negative effects, such as targeted human-led code inspections or increased in-person collaboration.
By starting a **cross-community conversation,** this work will help **dRTP leaders** adopt AI thoughtfully and intentionally. The goal is to ensure that the role of technical leaders remains recognised and valued.
