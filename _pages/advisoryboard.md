<section>
  <h2>Introduction</h2>
  <p>
    The <strong>DisCouRSE Network+</strong> project will encourage and support the development of leaders across all
    digital Research Technical Professional (RTP) roles, resulting in an empowered and connected community equipped to
    shape the future of digital research.
  </p>
  <p>
    A key activity will be the disbursement of a <strong>£1 million flexible fund</strong> in a series of five calls over
    the 3.5-year lifetime of the project. An independent Advisory Board will provide external oversight and steering
    for DisCouRSE. This document sets out the expectations for the Board.
  </p>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Responsibilities</h2>
  <p>Members of the Advisory Board agree to:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Review the progress of the Network against its objectives;</li>
    <li>Review and advise on plans for the flexible fund, in particular the timing, budget, and focus area of each call;</li>
    <li>Help to prioritise joint working between the DRI Network+ grants and related activities;</li>
    <li>Propose members of expert review panels to ensure independent scrutiny of our approach to distributing funds;</li>
    <li>Act as a bridge between the Network and their own communities, helping to disseminate calls and maximise impact.</li>
  </ul>
  <p>
    If desired, members may also review details of unsuccessful proposals to the flexible fund (where they are not
    conflicted) as an additional check against bias.
  </p>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Membership</h2>
  <p>
    The membership and Terms of Reference of the Advisory Board will be reviewed annually, although we hope that Board
    members will continue for the duration of the project. Membership will consist of figures representing different
    digital RTP communities, external to the project partner institutions.
  </p>
  <p>
    A diverse Board will be sought, covering a range of geographical areas, career stages, role types, and genders. The
    Board will have eight members. The initial list of invitees is as follows:
  </p>

  <ul>

    <li>Stephen Larson, CEO, Metacell</li>
    <li>Martyn Guest, Director of ARC, Cardiff (and SHARING AI Skills Hub co-lead)</li>
    <li>Caroline Ang, Director of Operations, IMI, Bath (and MInDS Network+ co-lead)</li>
    <li>James McLaughlin, Professor of Biomedical Engineering, Ulster University</li>
    <li>Saul Cozens, Head of Delivery for Research and Innovation IT, Sheffield (and Chair of RUGRIT)</li>
    <li>Fleur Hughes, PRISM Manager, York</li>
  </ul>

  <p><strong>Observer:</strong> Charlotte Clarke – NERC UKRI</p>

  <p>
    The Board will elect a Chair and Deputy Chair from among their members at their first meeting, or adopt a rolling
    Chair arrangement, changing each meeting, to oversee discussion and keep meetings to time.
  </p>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Frequency and Composition of Meetings</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>The Advisory Board will meet virtually twice a year. Interim meetings may be called by the Chair if required.</li>
    <li>The meeting will be deemed quorate if at least five Board members are present.</li>
    <li>
      The DisCouRSE Network Manager will attend Advisory Board meetings to take brief minutes. Other members of the
      DisCouRSE project team will attend if called on by the Board.
    </li>
  </ul>
</section>
